Understanding Light Exposure & Sleep relationship

The circadian rhythm, our body's internal clock, influences our sleep patterns, energy levels, and overall health. Light exposure, both natural and artificial, plays a crucial role in regulating this rhythm. This guide is based on the research of neuroscientist Dr. Andrew Huberman and chronobiologist Dr. Samer Hattar.

Wake up later

Exposure to light during the "red zone" hours can delay your circadian rhythm, causing you to wake up later.

Wake up sooner

Light exposure during the "yellow zone" hours can advance your circadian rhythm, leading you to wake up earlier.

Maintain schedule

Consistent light exposure during the "green zone" hours will help stabilize your circadian rhythm and maintain your sleep schedule.

Dead zone

During the "grey zone" hours, your body is less responsive to light exposure.
