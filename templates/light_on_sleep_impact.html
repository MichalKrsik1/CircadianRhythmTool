Strategies

  • Engage with light during the hours you desire to be awake.
  • Limit light exposure during the hours you want to rest or prepare for sleep.

Helpful Hints

How much light is needed?

100k Lux, equivalent to about 5-15 minutes of sunlight exposure (depending on weather and location), is recommended. Artificial light is also effective but generally emits less lux.

How to manage light exposure?

It's not necessary to completely avoid light. During evenings, aim to reduce light to a level where you can just about manage to see. Your eyes can adjust to much lower light levels than you might expect.

Impact of food and exercise

Hard exercise and heavy meals can have a similar effect to light on your circadian rhythm. Same rules apply for when to seek / avoid them as with light.

Sleep cycles

For a more refreshing wake-up, consider timing it with the right sleep cycle stage, even if it's earlier than planned."
